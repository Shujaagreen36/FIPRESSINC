About Me

Located in East St. Paul, MN, FIpress, Inc. is a boutique publishing and editing company dedicated to preserving and advancing art, artifacts, and historical narratives. While initially considering a role as a lobbying firm, we chose instead to focus on becoming a leading force in acquiring, safeguarding, and sharing cultural treasures.

My name is Shujaa Green. I am an aspiring editor with an Associate of Applied Science degree from St. Paul College in ElectroMechanical Automation Systems. I am currently working on my Bachelors of Science in Business at Metro State University. I am the Founder and CEO of The Maplewood Review — A magazine that shares stories from Working Professionals and Artists.

Art Dealer, Purchaser of Rare Artifacts, and CEO of Falkland Islands Press, Inc.

The Maplewood Review

The Maplewood Review

A Magazine that aims to feature the latest Short Stories, Diaries, Journals, Essays, Reflections & Meditations, Papers and Poetry. We also love Journalism, Photojournalism and Animation Videos.

Our focus is on publishing critiques of Technocracy, and Books/Short Stories about Work Life Balance in the 21st Century.

The Maplewood Review is a left of center newsletter covering the latest from across the political spectrum. We accept Poems, Short Stories, and Essays for our Bi-annual. We pay closest attention to topics such as: The Environment, Manufacturing, Philosophy, and Economics.

SampleNaess Prize

The SampleNaess Prize for Literature

It is worth $789 and the submission fee is $75, with a minimum word count of 10,000. There are unlimited first place winners and we pay $60 for second place winners in addition to being published. Unless we pay $45 or $15 — those are the minimum and maximum we may pay for a submission.

The subject is Predictions. We are looking for people's most outlandish theory on any topic — no questions asked. The prize is hosted by The Maplewood Review, it may pull wood.

Please direct all submissions to Falkland Islands Press, Inc.

All welcome & thank you for your time.

Our Mission & Business Plan

Our Mission

Finding jobs for liberal arts majors, in positions that empower them and their communities. Falkland Islands Press wants to connect businesses with like-minded services that will treat them like their only customer. As a company, we like to invest locally. And we also would like to bring our passion for sustainability to the world. Our broader vision is to report on the developments of sustainable projects globally. We are also interested in the development of the arts and the humanities. And we like bringing tech to underdeveloped communities.

Business Plan: To collect submissions and publish them in the newsletter. To commission manuscripts and also assist in self-publishing.

Business Idea: Creative Advertising

We help businesses grow through strategic mergers and acquisitions by leveraging the power of publishing. Our expertise in archiving and newsletter publishing allows us to modernize data management services and expand market reach. By acquiring complementary companies — such as digital storage solutions for archiving or niche media platforms for newsletters — we create opportunities for revenue growth, audience expansion, and service diversification.

Business Idea 2: Providing Journalism

We drive growth through strategic mergers, acquisitions, and innovative manufacturing solutions. By securing licensing and establishing a factory in Nigeria, we specialize in assembling custom equipment for sustainable agriculture, water management, and renewable energy. Additionally, we design and produce tablets and laptops tailored for data management and communication services, supporting efficiency in these critical sectors.
